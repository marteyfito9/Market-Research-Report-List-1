<p><p>The arm splint market is categorized primarily into two types: static splints and dynamic splints. Static splints are designed to immobilize injured or post-surgical arms, providing support and stability during the healing process. They are often made from rigid materials. In contrast, dynamic splints allow for controlled movement while still providing support, often using elastic components to facilitate rehabilitation. Both types serve essential roles in injury management, post-operative care, and rehabilitation, catering to varying needs in therapeutic settings.</p></p>
