<p><p>Intraoral X-Ray systems are vital for dental diagnostics, divided into two main types: digital and analog. Digital X-ray imaging utilizes electronic sensors to capture and display images instantly on a computer, offering enhanced image quality, reduced radiation exposure, and easier storage and sharing. In contrast, analog X-ray imaging relies on traditional film to produce images, requiring chemical processing, which can be time-consuming and less efficient. Both types play crucial roles in dental practices, influencing workflow and patient care.</p></p>
