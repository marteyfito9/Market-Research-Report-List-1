<p><p>Cataphoretic dip coatings involve an electrochemical process where charged particles are deposited onto a substrate immersed in a coating solution. In the cathodic market, the coating adheres to negatively charged surfaces, providing enhanced corrosion resistance, often used on automotive and industrial components. The anodic market, in contrast, focuses on positively charged surfaces, offering different coating properties and applications, like aesthetic finishes or specialized protective layers. Both types cater to diverse industries requiring durable and efficient surface treatments.</p></p>
